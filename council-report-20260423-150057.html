The Question The frontend-design skill proposed 7 improvements to push the Better Details Sta. Maria site further — editorial watermark numerals, hexagon motif, motorsport spec-sheet hero, editorial services spine, before/after slider, pull-quote reviews, and a custom chrome-ring cursor. Audience is Filipino premium-car owners arriving ~80% on mobile via Google Maps. Which ideas actually convert vs. look impressive in isolation? Which to drop, keep, modify?

Chairman's Recommendation

Ship a stripped v1, not the magazine redesign. Four of five advisors (and all 5 peer reviewers) landed on the same pattern: most of the design-push feels like magazine cosplay for a brand-new shop still earning legitimacy. The one feature that actually sells paint protection — the before/after slider — should be the first thing you ship, not the last.

The One Thing to Do First

Ship the Before/After drag-slider before anything else. It's the only proposal that directly answers "are you legit?" for a Maps-arriving mobile user — the single question that matters.

Where the Council Clashed

The editorial services spine (#4)

Sharpest disagreement in the session:

Blind Spots That Emerged Only in Peer Review

Every peer reviewer independently flagged the same set of gaps the 5 advisors missed:

  • Google Business Profile is the actual top of funnel — for Maps-arriving users, GBP photos, hours, the "Book" button, and FB Messenger auto-reply do more conversion work than the website will
  • No Tagalog/Taglish copy — site is English-only for a Filipino audience
  • Viber/Messenger are the dominant PH channels, not WhatsApp or web forms
  • Load performance on Philippine 4G — every unnecessary font/SVG costs data
  • LocalBusiness schema markup — so the site reinforces (not competes with) the GBP listing
  • Pricing transparency ("starts at ₱X") — a known premium-market trust signal in PH
  • 4.9★/77 reviews should be pulled live or semantically marked up, not retyped
